About Me

I'm a writer by trade, I love language. That love of language has led me through an advertising and marketing program at Centennial college where I developed it further to be useful in business and marketing.

Shortly after the end of the program I started doing web development and a little graphic design. I found lots of work in the realm of cryptocurrency (Bitcoin, etc.) and worked here and there doing more or less the same kind of work followed by two internships in the field of marketing and public relations at two of Toronto's award winning agencies.

When I'm not perusing a monetary reward for my work, I'm usually browsing the internet looking for new and interesting things to learn about. From screen printing to woodworking and electronic engineering, I really enjoy finding out how to do things and find creative solutions to problems. One of the more recent ones has been learning to speak, read, and write in Esperanto; a constructed international auxiliary language that has around 2 million speakers worldwide.

I also maintain a small blog on Medium for those looking for a variety of topics, but usually it's just random things I want to write about.
