About Zach

~ The Cake Was a Lie ~

But

while you're here you might as well get to know me a little better. For starters my passion for all things digital has allowed me to diversify my copywriting and marketing skills to cater specifically to the online world. I love to find creative solutions to problems whether it be a catchy headline or what content to use for a landing page.


I am in every sense of the word a nerd as there's nothing I'd rather do on a Friday afternoon than learn something obscure or downright weird. Hence the label "'Zach' of all trades" as my habit of continually absorbing information has left me with a wide range of random abilities and knowledge that may or may not be useful; but I'm more than happy to show them off if needed!


So why call myself a digital marketing specialist if I have a random skill set? Believe it or not they've all been useful in the past. For example, lock picking taught me to take my time with a complex problem. Where as learning to day trade cryptocurrencies (Bitcoin, Ethereum, etc.) was later useful when I started bidding on keywords for Google AdWords campaigns.


My previous work experiences have taught me a lot about the wonderful world of marketing. The vast majority of which can be summed up in content strategy and a little graphic design on the side. One thing I absolutely love about each of them was the research and planning components and seeing the end results.


And then there's my various side hobbies include biking, web design, creative writing, and just walking around Toronto snapping Instagrams of city life. I also play around with Photoshop and Illustrator a lot when I get "the artsy itch". Usually the products of my labour are Toronto related, such as a Love Letter to the SRT or a TTC parody map which was featured on the Torontoist and on Global News.


Then there's my fascination with the international auxiliary language known as Esperanto. In fact, currently I'm working an Esperanto writing project called "Kvindek Du Semajnoj de Esperanto" or "52 Weeks of Esperanto". It's exactly what it sounds like, one blog post in Esperanto each week for one year... Fun!
